# Semut Narrative

> What remains human when we progressively outsource our capabilities?

Semut Narrative is the philosophical and conceptual foundation of the Semut project.

It does not attempt to provide a final answer.

Instead, it continually asks one question:

> What is a human?

Throughout history, humans have continuously extended themselves through technology.

- Stone tools extended strength.
- Language extended cooperation.
- Writing extended memory.
- Machines extended labor.
- Computers extended calculation.
- The Internet extended information.
- Artificial Intelligence may extend reasoning.

As more capabilities move outside ourselves, what remains fundamentally human?

Semut begins from this question.

Rather than defining humanity from a single philosophical tradition, Semut explores ideas across philosophy, biology, anthropology, cognitive science, history, engineering, and other disciplines.

The goal is not to replace these perspectives, but to explore whether they can be understood within a common coordination framework.

This repository contains essays, presentations, diagrams, videos, and educational materials that explain the ideas behind Semut.
